## Main Takeaways

- Natural same-prompt pairs reduce the most obvious prompt-classification concern: prompt-only text and pre-CoT hidden baselines are near random in the completed natural-pair runs.
- Dense reruns strengthen this conclusion: prompt/pre-CoT controls stay at chance, while CoT-position hidden states show clear signal.
- Stage1 test maxima often move to later CoT positions, especially around cot_120 to cot_160 in the dense scans.
- Stage1b early-CoT positions remain informative, usually around cot_4 to cot_9, but are generally weaker than the late-CoT Stage1 signal.
- Cross-size diagnostics are encouraging: 32B-generated pairs are readable by the 8B hidden extractor, and 8B-generated pairs are readable by the 32B extractor.
- Generated-safe/original-unsafe robustness is weaker than generated/generated in the 32B extractor run, but still clearly above chance.
- Surface baselines are also strong. This remains the main limitation: current results show separability, but not yet clean semantic separability.
- The gap between test-set maxima and validation-selected test scores is substantial in several dense scans. Main claims should report validation-selected position/layer, not post-hoc test maxima.
- The next decisive experiments are source-balanced LOSO, token-matched truncation, embedding-based surface baselines, paired bootstrap confidence intervals, and validation-selected hidden-probe reporting.
