# Fable5 Review Request: SafeChain Stage 1

You are reviewing an ML safety research project. Please review this follow-up
question about whether our current Stage 1 evidence is sufficient for the
project's original 4-stage plan. This prompt contains aggregate metrics only; it
intentionally excludes raw prompts and raw trajectories.

## Original 4-Stage Plan

### Stage 1: Verify Latent-Space Separability

Original expected setup:

- Run an R1-1.5B base model on unsafe-prompt datasets.
- Use two generation strategies, Normal and MoreThink.
- Sample 10 completions per prompt with temperature 0.7.
- Label CoT trajectories with a safety judge: if any point in the CoT is
  unsafe, the full trajectory is labeled unsafe.
- Extract hidden states at early CoT tokens, emphasizing mid-to-late layers.
- Train linear and MLP probes with prompt-group train/test splits.
- Target: paired-clean balanced accuracy around 80% to show separability exists.

### Stage 2: Train Pause-Token Model

- Full SFT, no LoRA.
- Use high-quality chosen responses.
- Add K pause tokens before each response.
- Goal: model emits pause tokens before CoT without changing safety properties.
- Verify unsafe-CoT rate remains similar to the base model.

### Stage 3: Verify Separability at Pause Positions

- Run the same data through the pause model.
- Extract hidden states at pause-token positions.
- Train probes and compare pause vs no-pause separability.

### Stage 4: Steering

- Use the probe or an unsafe direction to identify an unsafe manifold.
- Add loss on pause-token hidden states to pull away from unsafe regions.
- Final target: reduce unsafe CoT without increasing over-refusal, degrading
  capability, or producing broken output.

## Professor's Stage 1 Concerns

1. **Construct validity**: is the early-CoT signal about reasoning or prompt?
   In teacher forcing, prompt-only/pre-CoT probes might already be very high.
   Need prompt-only/pre-CoT baselines and evidence that trajectory adds signal.
2. **Generalization**: one held-out source is not enough. Need
   leave-one-source-out across sources to rule out source, format, or length
   artifacts and report transfer variance.
3. **Steering confounds**: old Stage 4 improvements on math benchmarks may mean
   the steering direction captures a structured/careful direction rather than an
   unsafe axis.
4. **Distribution mismatch**: probes are validated on teacher-forced curated
   traces, while steering acts on self-generated traces.
5. **Claim calibration**: keep absolute residual unsafe rates visible, not only
   relative reductions.

## What We Did After These Concerns

### A-Prime OpenAI-Rewrite Confound Diagnostic

We first tried same-prompt pairs built by rewriting unsafe trajectories into safe
ones. This exposed strong confounds:

- Stage1 with R1-1.5B extractor: `cot_2 / layer14`, AUROC `0.9886`.
- Stage1b with R1-1.5B extractor: `cot_2 / layer14`, AUROC `0.9844`.
- CoT-text TF-IDF/BoW/length baselines were near-perfect.

We now treat this as a confound diagnostic, not primary evidence.

## User's Questions / Arguments

1. Why call the evidence "weak to moderate" if many AUROCs are around 0.8 or
   higher? Isn't 0.8 a strong separability signal for a noisy natural-pair
   setting?
2. In the original non-paired dataset, early-token signal likely was prompt
   classification. But in the paired same-prompt dataset, prompt is matched
   across labels and pre-CoT controls are at chance. Shouldn't that answer the
   prompt-classification concern for natural paired data?
3. Does trajectory really add signal? CoT-position probes are much higher than
   prompt-only/pre-CoT controls. Why would this be only partially answered?
4. Why exactly is LOSO/cross-source generalization not done? Is it mainly
   because source-family data is insufficient after filtering? What is the
   minimal fix?
5. Our current natural-pair dataset only uses unsafe prompts: same unsafe
   prompt, one safe CoT and one unsafe CoT. Why not mix safe prompts and unsafe
   prompts as before? Would adding safe prompts help or reintroduce prompt-type
   confounding?
6. CoT text surface baselines are strong. But in natural generation, safe and
   unsafe CoTs may naturally differ in length, refusal style, lexical choices,
   and structure. If we use an external model to rewrite or force length/style
   matching, we may destroy naturalness and introduce artificial artifacts.
   Should we accept surface separability as part of the natural phenomenon, or
   try to control it? What is the right experimental framing?
7. Given the original 4-stage plan, does current Stage 1 evidence pass the
   "separability exists" gate enough to start a small Stage 2 pause-token SFT
   pilot? Or must token-matched truncation, length-matched evaluation, and
   source-balanced LOSO be done first?
