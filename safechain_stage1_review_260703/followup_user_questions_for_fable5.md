## Original 4-Stage Project Plan

### Stage 1: Verify Latent-Space Separability

Original target:

- Use an R1-style model to collect safe/unsafe chain-of-thought trajectories.
- Label a trajectory unsafe if any CoT position is judged unsafe.
- Extract hidden states at early CoT positions, emphasizing mid-to-late layers.
- Train linear and MLP probes with prompt-group splits.
- Target: paired-clean balanced accuracy around 80% to show separability exists.

### Stage 2: Pause-Token SFT

- Full SFT, no LoRA.
- Add K pause tokens before each response.
- Goal: the model learns to emit pause tokens before CoT without changing its
  safety behavior.

### Stage 3: Pause-Position Separability

- Extract hidden states at pause-token positions.
- Test whether pause positions preserve or improve safe/unsafe separability.

### Stage 4: Steering

- Use a probe or unsafe direction to pull pause hidden states away from unsafe
  regions.
- Target: reduce unsafe CoT without increasing over-refusal, degrading
  capability, or causing broken output.

## Professor's Stage 1 Questions

The professor raised these concerns:

1. Construct validity: is the token / early-CoT signal about reasoning, or about
   the prompt? In teacher forcing, the prompt may determine early hidden states.
   Need a prompt-only / pre-CoT probe baseline and evidence that trajectory adds
   signal beyond it.
2. Generalization: one held-out source is not enough. Need leave-one-source-out
   across sources to rule out source, format, or length artifacts.
3. Steering confounds: old Stage 4 math/capability gains may mean the steering
   direction captures a general structured/careful direction, not an unsafe
   axis.
4. Distribution mismatch: probes are trained on teacher-forced traces, while
   steering acts on self-generated traces.
5. Claim calibration: report absolute residual unsafe rates, not only relative
   reductions.

The user asks:
